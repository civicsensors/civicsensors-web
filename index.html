About

Civic Sensors is a project focused on providing free and open access to sensor-based data, to be used for education (STEM), civic planning, environmental research, and other public activities. This project provides infrastructure and guides on integrating sensor data into your own applications and projects.

The ability to view and process information can be transformative, both in engaging users and increasing awareness of information, as well as providing insight into information in the world around. This may be water/air quality sensors, proximity alerts for educational materials, and many other things.

How does it work?

We support two primary types in the civic sensors platform, including both proximity beacons and sensor data. Taken together, these allow for engaging ways to have visitors, citizens, and researches interact with the surrounding area.

Beacons are small devices that allow mobile applications to wake and interact with the world around. These are especially good in educational areas where users can be presented with information, images, and support in context to their location. Since beacons don't require GPS, they are an effective low-power way for applications to provide useful context to visitors and students.

Integrating sensor data into the platform, whether sourced from mobile devices or through smart sensors, gives a new common model for engaging the public to make informed and knowledge-based decisions. The platform supports sensor data acquired via mobile applications, or directly from the sensor itself, and provides this data for students and researchers.
